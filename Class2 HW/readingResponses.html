Final Project Reflection


For the final project, I decide to create a hand-code portfolio site for the projects I have done before. I struggled a lot at first as I was trying to decide if I want to create a narrative storytelling website or a practical portfolio website as the final project. As struggling for a while, I believe that a portfolio website may be a better practice and showcase for the technical skill I have learned throughout this class. Besides, this is one of my motivations and goal for taking this class.

I met a few difficulties during the process of coding this website. I struggled a lot when designing the layout and spent some time learning how to code it in the way I want, for instance, the numbers and sizes of rows and columns and the space between them. I also struggled with the font, font size, and line space between them. The most important thing for me to consider is the affordance and readability of the website. After figuring out the layout, I began working on the interaction with each section and element of the website, such as how can the navigation text be a clear indicator of where you are and how to show which image or text can be clicked on. I chose to use the hover effect for the text and image for better reading and interaction experience. The color of the text will change and the project images would drop 3D shadow when the mouse moves over.

This site is still incomplete and may never be complete as this is a place I will continue to store my future work.

Class 4 Server: Reading Response


Frank Chimero’s What Screens Want is a very insightful reading. I’m deeply inspired by the discussion and exploration of the screen and the concepts he introduces. As Chimero states, “screens are aesthetically neutral.” It is a medium or interface that would not limit the thing that is presented or created inside it. I found the discussion about metaphors and abstractions in this reading quite insightful. Metaphors and abstractions may help people understand things better but may also create misinformation, distortion, or biases. I somehow related to Chimero’s worries, since the internet industry developed and changed so frequently that before most users haven’t clearly understood how the webs work and develop, it seems settled now and limits its possibilities. The web we are seeing right now is lack variations and contains bias and assumptions. We shall develop more perspectives ways and aims in viewing and using the web.

Besides, after reading this article, it makes me wonder that the screen may also be a temporarily popular medium/material that is widely used in the current age because it now has the best flux, as Chimero referred to, that we need and want now. If, in the future, a new and more effective medium is invented for handling abstractions, changes, and transformations, how will the screen be seen and placed?

In Chancey Fleet’s Dark Patterns in Accessibility Tech talk, she illustrates the importance of accessibility features in the digital ecosystem. This talk reminds me of something I learned from another class a couple of weeks ago, Accessibility features are not only useful for disabled people. All people are actually using the advantage and convenience of the accessible feature. I completely agree with Chancey’s statement that we should change the “perception that accessibility features are for disabled people” and realize that “we share one ecosystem and they are for everyone.” Accessibility features should not be viewed as edge cases but as infrastructure. The inclusion of accessibility technology during development is critical not only to disabled people and their interests but will develop a more equitable and better digital ecosystem for all people. Reading Links:

Reading Links:

American Artist, Black Gooey Universe
Chancey Fleet Dark Patterns in Accessibility Tech
Critical Interface Manifesto

Class 3 Cyberfeminism: Reading Response


In Protocol, written by Alexander Galloway, the Introduction chapter examines the development of computer protocol and explains some commonly used concepts related to the internet protocol. This reading helps me a lot in learning why and how the internet/website is a political space as the professor mentioned in the first class. Galloway argues that the founding principle of the internet and related technologies is not freedom, but control, which makes me realize that there is a management and power distribution system that lies behind the technical protocols. Do we, the individuals, have more power and freedom in the current society with easy access to the internet and related technologies or we have been put in a massive invisible power net that control by the authorities? Does an individual truly have control of their information? What is the actual relationship between the individual and the web? These are the questions that bother me for a long time. I am trying to learn more about the political relationship between digital media and technology through readings and practice.

Feminist Server Summit Afterlife is an informative female server manifesto. I am deeply impressed and inspired by some of its statements, such as “does not confuse safety with security” and “…Is autonomous in the sense that she decides for her own dependencies.” This manifesto provides strong statements about cyberfeminism and concepts of the female server.

Reading Links:
Alexander Galloway, 2004, Protocol
Feminist Server Manifesto: A Feminist Servr Manifesto 0.01

Class 1 Welcome: Reading Response


When reading J.R Carpenter’s A Handmade Web, I found it interesting that the handmade web is closely linked to the creator and a person’s identity and how people view handmade web and public big sites differently. They host the creator’s identity in personal ways and are one of a kind. The handmade web has more intimate and quieter. The emotions contained in a handmade web seem to be more obvious and the connections between people and handmade we are stronger. Reading this article makes me realize a phenomenon that not only happens in the internet world but exists in the entire human development history. People always tend to start from something small and intimate to something big, popular, and crowded, whereas, somehow, in the end, people would always find a way to slow down and go back to the initial idea with something quieter and smaller.

In Olia Lillian’s “Vernacular Web”, the concept and statement about translucency, transparency, and how developers and users perceive the web provides me with a new way of thinking about the current usage and functionality of the web. I believe I am one of the amateurs and users that almost know nothing about how a web is crafted or functions as Olia mentioned in the article. I used to think that an efficient and understandable interface is a good and necessary quality, but now I am not sure if this is still a good thing for the user or if it imperceptibly enlarges the gap between the user and understanding the interface and the mindset between designers and users. Besides, It is quite interesting to learn that web2.0 has more attraction or features but still follows some rules and traditions from web1.0 and non-digital mediums.

When reading Laurel Schwulst’s article, I really like and agree with how Laurel Schwulst describes the website as “they’re both subject and object at once. In other words, a website creator becomes both author and architect simultaneously.” I completely agree with Laurel Schwulst’s opinion that the “web needs to actually work for people instead of being powered by a small handful of big corporations.” It makes me start thinking of the power dynamics within the web, and the relationship between developers and users. I am inspired by how Laurel Schwulst uses metaphors to demonstrate how flexible a website can be and what world people can create with its possibilities and potential for each individual.

Reading Links:
J. R. CARPENTER, 2015 A Handmade Web
Olia Lialina, 2012, Vernacular Web 2
Laurel Schwulst, 2018, My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?
